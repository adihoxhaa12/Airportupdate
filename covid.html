Covid 19 rules:
Maintain a safe distance (usually 1-2 meters/3-6 feet) from others, especially in crowded places.
Avoid large gatherings and stay away from places with high-risk exposure.
Wear face masks in public places, especially indoors, on public transportation, or where social distancing is difficult.
Masks help reduce the spread of respiratory droplets that may carry the virus.
Wash hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds.
If soap and water are not available, use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.
Getting vaccinated against COVID-19 is highly encouraged where vaccines are available.
If experiencing symptoms of COVID-19 or exposed to someone who tested positive, get tested immediately.
Follow local guidelines for quarantine or isolation if you test positive or have been in close contact with someone who has tested positive.
Many countries have implemented travel restrictions, such as mandatory quarantine upon arrival, proof of negative COVID-19 tests, or vaccination certificates.
Check travel advisories for updates on international or domestic travel guidelines.
Regularly clean and disinfect commonly touched surfaces, such as doorknobs, light switches, and electronics.
High-touch public areas may have increased cleaning and sanitation protocols.
Follow local rules and regulations, as they can vary widely depending on the region. Some areas might enforce stricter measures, like curfews, while others might be more relaxed.
Take care of your mental health by staying connected with loved ones, maintaining a routine, and seeking support if needed.
